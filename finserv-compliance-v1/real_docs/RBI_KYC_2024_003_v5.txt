RESERVE BANK OF INDIA
MASTER DIRECTION — KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC)
RBI/2016-17/23 — As Updated through Circular RBI/2024-25/47
Version: 2024-v5 | Effective: 1 April 2024

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Short Title and Commencement
These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Know Your Customer)
Directions, 2016, as amended up to 2024, and shall be applicable to all Regulated
Entities (REs) as defined herein.

2. Definitions

2.1 "Regulated Entity" (RE) means all commercial banks, small finance banks, payment
banks, cooperative banks, NBFCs, and other financial institutions regulated by RBI.

2.2 "Customer Due Diligence" (CDD) means identifying and verifying the identity of
the customer and beneficial owner, understanding the nature and purpose of the business
relationship, and ongoing monitoring of the business relationship.

2.3 "Politically Exposed Person" (PEP) means individuals who are or have been entrusted
with prominent public functions domestically or by a foreign country, including heads of
state, senior politicians, senior government or judicial officials, senior executives of
state-owned corporations, and senior military officials.

2.4 "Beneficial Owner" (BO) means the natural person(s) who ultimately own(s) or
control(s) a customer and/or the natural person on whose behalf a transaction is
being conducted; ownership threshold: >25% for companies, >15% for partnerships/trusts.

CHAPTER II — CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3. Customer Acceptance Policy

3.1 Risk-Based Approach
REs shall adopt a Risk-Based Approach (RBA) to KYC and categorise customers as
LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH risk. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) is mandatory for HIGH-risk
customers including:
  (a) Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their immediate family members;
  (b) Customers in high-risk countries/jurisdictions as notified by FATF;
  (c) Customers with complex ownership structures;
  (d) Non-resident customers.

3.2 Prohibited Accounts
REs must not open accounts for:
  (a) Anonymous or fictitious persons;
  (b) Entities listed under UN sanctions or those on RBI/Government watch lists;
  (c) Persons/entities for whom CDD cannot be completed.

4. Customer Identification Procedure (CIP)

4.1 Individual Customers
For individuals, REs must obtain and verify:
  (a) Officially Valid Document (OVD): Aadhaar / Passport / Driving Licence / Voter ID;
  (b) Permanent Account Number (PAN) or Form 60 where PAN is not available;
  (c) Recent photograph.

4.2 Legal Entities
For companies, trusts, and partnerships, REs must verify:
  (a) Certificate of incorporation / registration document;
  (b) Memorandum and Articles of Association;
  (c) PAN of the entity;
  (d) Identity and address of beneficial owners (>25% ownership or control).

4.3 Digital KYC
Video-based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP) is permitted as an alternative to
in-person verification subject to the technical and procedural requirements prescribed
in Annex I of these Directions.

5. Periodic Updation

5.1 Re-KYC Frequency
REs shall undertake periodic updation of KYC records:
  (a) HIGH-risk customers: every 2 years;
  (b) MEDIUM-risk customers: every 8 years;
  (c) LOW-risk customers: every 10 years.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CHAPTER III — ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

6.1 Mandatory Senior Management Approval
Accounts of PEPs must be approved by senior management (at least DGM level or equivalent)
before opening. This requirement applies both to PEPs who are direct customers and those
who are beneficial owners.

6.2 Source of Funds
REs must satisfy themselves about the legitimacy of the source of funds for PEP accounts
and document this assessment in the account file.

6.3 Ongoing Monitoring
PEP accounts must be subject to enhanced ongoing monitoring including:
  (a) More frequent transaction reviews;
  (b) Annual re-assessment of risk classification;
  (c) Real-time transaction alerts for unusual patterns.

7. Correspondent Banking

7.1 Before Establishing Correspondent Relationships
REs must obtain sufficient information about the respondent institution to understand
fully the nature of its business, including:
  (a) Ownership and management structure;
  (b) Regulatory status in its home jurisdiction;
  (c) AML/CFT controls — the RE must not establish or continue correspondent relationships
      with shell banks.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CHAPTER IV — RECORD KEEPING AND REPORTING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8. Record Maintenance

8.1 Transaction Records
REs must maintain all transaction records for a minimum of five (5) years from the date
of the transaction. Records must be sufficient to reconstruct individual transactions and
provide an audit trail for prosecution where required.

8.2 Customer Identification Records
KYC records of customers must be maintained for at least five (5) years after the
business relationship has ended.

9. Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)

9.1 Obligation to Report
REs must report to the Financial Intelligence Unit — India (FIU-IND) any transaction
that gives rise to reasonable grounds to suspect that it may be related to money
laundering, financing of terrorism, or any other offence within 7 working days of
forming that suspicion.

9.2 Tipping-Off Prohibition
REs and their employees must NOT disclose ("tip off") to the customer or any third party
that a suspicious transaction report has been filed or is being filed.
