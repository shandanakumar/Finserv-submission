MARKETS IN FINANCIAL INSTRUMENTS DIRECTIVE II (MiFID II)
Consolidated Reference — Key Compliance Requirements for Investment Firms
Source: European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) / European Commission
Regulation: Directive 2014/65/EU as amended | Version: 2024-v2 | Effective: 1 March 2024

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TITLE I — CLIENT CLASSIFICATION AND SUITABILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Article 1: Client Classification

1.1 Categories of Clients
Investment firms must classify clients into one of three categories:
  (a) Retail Clients — afforded the highest level of protection;
  (b) Professional Clients per se — meet criteria in Annex II of MiFID II;
  (c) Eligible Counterparties — lowest protection level; limited to specific entity types.

1.2 Reclassification
Firms must have documented procedures allowing clients to request reclassification and
must assess whether the request is appropriate given the client's knowledge and experience.

Article 2: Suitability Assessment

2.1 Obligation to Assess Suitability
When providing investment advice or portfolio management services, a firm must obtain
the necessary information regarding the client's:
  (a) Knowledge and experience in the investment field;
  (b) Financial situation including ability to bear losses;
  (c) Investment objectives including risk tolerance.

2.2 Suitability Report
Firms must provide retail clients with a suitability report prior to (or contemporaneously
with) executing a recommended transaction. The report must specify how the recommendation
meets the client's personal circumstances.

2.3 Non-Complex Products — Execution Only
For non-complex products transacted on a client's own initiative (execution-only),
the suitability assessment obligation does not apply. However, the firm must:
  (a) Clearly inform the client that no suitability check is performed;
  (b) Satisfy itself that the product is non-complex under Article 57 of MiFID II RTS.

2.4 Complex Products — Enhanced Obligations
For complex products (structured notes, derivatives, funds with leverage), a full suitability
assessment is MANDATORY for retail clients regardless of service type. Firms must not
proceed with the transaction if a positive suitability determination cannot be made.

Article 3: Appropriateness Assessment

3.1 Appropriateness for Non-Advised Services
Where a firm provides services other than investment advice or portfolio management
in respect of complex products, it must assess whether the client has the necessary
knowledge and experience to understand the risks.

3.2 Warning Obligation
If the assessment reveals that the product is not appropriate, the firm must warn the
client. If the client insists on proceeding, the firm may execute but must document
the warning given.

TITLE II — BEST EXECUTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Article 4: Best Execution Obligation

4.1 General Requirement
Investment firms must take all sufficient steps to obtain the best possible result for
clients when executing orders, taking into account:
  (a) Price;
  (b) Costs;
  (c) Speed;
  (d) Likelihood of execution and settlement;
  (e) Size, nature, or any other consideration relevant to order execution.

4.2 Best Execution Policy
Firms must establish and implement a written best execution policy, reviewed at least
annually, setting out execution venues considered for each class of financial instrument.

4.3 Monitoring and Reporting
Firms must monitor the effectiveness of their order execution arrangements and publish
annual reports summarising the top five execution venues per instrument class (RTS 28).

TITLE III — PRODUCT GOVERNANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Article 5: Product Governance — Manufacturer Obligations

5.1 Target Market Definition
Manufacturers must identify a target market for each financial product and ensure the
product is designed to meet the needs of that target market. The target market assessment
must cover:
  (a) Type of client (retail, professional, eligible counterparty);
  (b) Clients' knowledge and experience;
  (c) Clients' financial situation (ability to bear losses);
  (d) Clients' risk tolerance;
  (e) Clients' objectives and needs.

5.2 Negative Target Market
Manufacturers must also identify categories of clients for whom the product is NOT
compatible (negative target market) and communicate this to distributors.

Article 6: Product Governance — Distributor Obligations

6.1 Distribution Strategy
Distributors must understand the product and its identified target market, and implement
a distribution strategy consistent with that target market. They must not target products
to clients falling within the negative target market.

6.2 Information Exchange
Distributors must provide manufacturers with sales data and information on client
complaints to enable ongoing product review.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TITLE IV — TRANSACTION REPORTING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Article 7: Transaction Reporting Obligations

7.1 Scope
Investment firms must report complete and accurate details of transactions in financial
instruments admitted to trading on a trading venue to their national competent authority
(NCA) no later than the close of the following working day (T+1).

7.2 Reportable Fields
Reports must include at minimum:
  (a) Trading date and time;
  (b) Instrument identification (ISIN / AII);
  (c) Quantity and price;
  (d) Counterparty and client identification (LEI, national ID);
  (e) Capacity (agent/principal);
  (f) Venue of execution.

7.3 LEI Requirement
Firms must obtain a Legal Entity Identifier (LEI) for all legal entity clients before
executing a transaction. "No LEI, no trade" is the prevailing ESMA guidance.
