MASTER DIRECTION — PRIORITY SECTOR LENDING (PSL)

As Updated through Circular RBI/2024-25/61
Version: 2024-v3 | Effective: 1 April 2024

CHAPTER I — TARGETS AND SUB-TARGETS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Applicability
These directions apply to all Scheduled Commercial Banks including Foreign Banks with
20 branches and above, Small Finance Banks (SFBs), and select NBFCs (NBFC-MFIs,
NBFC-Factors, HFCs) as applicable.

2. Overall PSL Target

2.1 Domestic Commercial Banks and Foreign Banks (20+ branches)
Total Priority Sector: 40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC) or Credit Equivalent
of Off-Balance Sheet Exposure (CEOBSE), whichever is higher.

2.2 Small Finance Banks
Total Priority Sector: 75% of ANBC.

3. Sub-Targets

3.1 Agriculture
Agriculture (including allied activities): 18% of ANBC.
  — Of this, 8% must flow to Small and Marginal Farmers (SMF).

3.2 Micro Enterprises
Micro Enterprises: 7.5% of ANBC.

3.3 Weaker Sections
Advances to Weaker Sections: 12% of ANBC.
Weaker sections include: Small and Marginal Farmers, Artisans, SCs/STs, Self Help
Groups, Distressed persons, Minority communities, Differently-abled persons.

CHAPTER II — ELIGIBLE CATEGORIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4. Agriculture

4.1 Farm Credit
Loans to individual farmers and joint liability groups (JLGs) for agriculture and
allied activities (dairy, fishery, animal husbandry, etc.) including short-term crop
loans, medium and long-term loans for agricultural purposes.
Loan limit: Up to ₹2 crore per individual borrower.

4.2 Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
KCC loans are eligible under the agriculture sub-target.

4.3 MSME — Agriculture Processing
Loans to MSMEs engaged in food and agro-processing with investment in plant and
machinery up to ₹50 crore.

5. Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSME)

5.1 Micro Enterprises
Loans to micro enterprises (investment ≤ ₹1 crore, turnover ≤ ₹5 crore).
No loan limit for PSL classification purposes.

5.2 Small Enterprises
Loans to small enterprises (investment ≤ ₹10 crore, turnover ≤ ₹50 crore).
Capped at ₹10 crore per borrower for PSL classification.

5.3 Khadi and Village Industries
All loans to KVI units classified as priority sector without any credit cap.

6. Export Credit

6.1 Incremental export credit over the corresponding date of the preceding year to
the extent it does not exceed 2% of ANBC is eligible under PSL for banks up to the
limit of ₹40 crore per borrower.

7. Education

7.1 Loans for educational purposes including vocational courses up to ₹20 lakh
(₹30 lakh for loans to students for studying abroad) are eligible.

8. Housing

8.1 Housing Loans
  (a) Loans up to ₹35 lakh in metropolitan centres (population ≥ 10 lakh) where
      dwelling unit cost does not exceed ₹45 lakh;
  (b) Loans up to ₹25 lakh in other centres where dwelling unit cost ≤ ₹30 lakh.

8.2 Slum Clearance
Bank loans for housing projects exclusively for economically weaker sections and
low-income groups; investment limit: ₹10 lakh per dwelling unit.

CHAPTER III — REPORTING AND SHORTFALL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9. Reporting to RBI

9.1 Quarterly Reporting
Banks must report their PSL achievement to RBI on a quarterly basis in the prescribed
format (Form A). The reference date for the last quarter of the financial year is
31 March.

9.2 Annual Certification
Banks must submit a certificate certified by their statutory auditors regarding PSL
achievement for the financial year by 30 June each year.

10. Shortfall — Priority Sector Lending Certificates (PSLCs) and RIDF

10.1 PSLC Trading
Banks with a PSL shortfall may purchase Priority Sector Lending Certificates (PSLCs)
from banks with surplus through the RBI-operated trading platform (e-Kuber) by the
last working day of March.

10.2 RIDF Contribution
Banks with a residual PSL shortfall (after PSLC purchases) must deposit the amount
in the Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) / other funds maintained by NABARD,
NHB, or SIDBI at below-market interest rates as notified by RBI each year.

10.3 Penalty Interest
The notional interest foregone on RIDF deposits constitutes the effective penalty for
non-achievement of PSL targets.
