BASEL III CAPITAL ADEQUACY FRAMEWORK
Consolidated Reference — Capital Requirements Regulation (CRR III)
Source: Bank for International Settlements / Basel Committee on Banking Supervision
Version: 2024-v1 | Effective: 1 January 2025

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART I — MINIMUM CAPITAL REQUIREMENTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Section 1: Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Capital

1.1 Minimum CET1 Ratio
Banks must maintain a minimum Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital ratio of 4.5% of
risk-weighted assets (RWAs) at all times. CET1 capital consists primarily of ordinary
shares and retained earnings that meet the strict eligibility criteria set out in Section 2.

1.2 Capital Conservation Buffer
In addition to the 4.5% CET1 minimum, banks must hold a Capital Conservation Buffer
(CCB) of 2.5% of RWAs, composed entirely of CET1 capital. Breaching the buffer triggers
automatic restrictions on distributions (dividends, share buybacks, discretionary bonuses).

1.3 Total Minimum CET1 Requirement
When combined, the minimum CET1 requirement including the conservation buffer is 7.0%
of risk-weighted assets.

Section 2: Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Capital

2.1 Minimum AT1 Requirement
The minimum Tier 1 capital ratio (CET1 + AT1) is set at 6.0% of RWAs.
AT1 instruments must meet all of the following criteria:
  (a) Perpetual with no maturity date;
  (b) Callable only at the initiative of the issuer, subject to supervisory approval;
  (c) Dividends/coupons fully discretionary and non-cumulative;
  (d) Loss absorption through write-down or conversion to common equity at a trigger event
      (CET1 ratio falling below 5.125%).

Section 3: Tier 2 Capital

3.1 Minimum Total Capital Ratio
The minimum total capital ratio (Tier 1 + Tier 2) is 8.0% of RWAs.
Tier 2 instruments must have an original maturity of at least five years and must not
include any incentive to redeem.

Section 4: Countercyclical Capital Buffer (CCyB)

4.1 Buffer Range
National authorities may impose a Countercyclical Capital Buffer ranging from 0% to 2.5%
of RWAs, applicable to banks' domestic credit exposures. The CCyB must be met with CET1
capital.

PART II — LARGE EXPOSURES FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Section 5: Large Exposure Limits

5.1 General Limit
A bank's exposure to a single counterparty or group of connected counterparties must not
exceed 25% of the bank's eligible capital base (Tier 1 capital) at any point in time.

5.2 G-SIB to G-SIB Exposures
For Global Systemically Important Banks (G-SIBs), the large exposure limit applicable to
exposures between G-SIBs is tightened to 15% of Tier 1 capital.

5.3 Intra-Group Exposures
Exposures to group entities are subject to the same 25% Tier 1 limit unless a specific
intra-group exemption has been granted by the competent authority. Banks must obtain
supervisory approval before breaching the intra-group sub-limit of 25%.

5.4 Reporting Obligation
Banks must report to their supervisory authority all large exposures exceeding 10% of
eligible capital on at least a quarterly basis.

PART III — LIQUIDITY COVERAGE RATIO (LCR)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Section 6: LCR Requirements

6.1 Minimum LCR
Banks must hold an adequate stock of unencumbered High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA)
to survive a significant liquidity stress scenario lasting 30 calendar days. The minimum
LCR is 100%.

   LCR = Stock of HQLA / Total Net Cash Outflows over 30 days ≥ 100%

6.2 HQLA Classification
Level 1 assets (cash, central bank reserves, sovereign bonds with 0% risk weight):
no haircut applied.
Level 2A assets (sovereign/agency bonds with 20% risk weight): 15% haircut.
Level 2B assets (certain corporate bonds, equities): 25–50% haircut; capped at 15%
of total HQLA.

Section 7: Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)

7.1 Minimum NSFR
Banks must maintain a minimum NSFR of 100% on an ongoing basis.

   NSFR = Available Stable Funding (ASF) / Required Stable Funding (RSF) ≥ 100%

7.2 Purpose
The NSFR limits over-reliance on short-term wholesale funding and promotes more stable,
longer-term funding structures. Supervisors may require a higher ratio for banks with
elevated structural liquidity risk.

PART IV — OPERATIONAL RISK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Section 8: Standardised Approach for Operational Risk (SA)

8.1 Business Indicator Component (BIC)
From 1 January 2025, the internal-models-based Advanced Measurement Approach (AMA)
is replaced by the single Standardised Approach. The Business Indicator (BI) is calculated
as the sum of:
  (a) Interest, leases, and dividends component;
  (b) Services component;
  (c) Financial component.

8.2 Internal Loss Multiplier (ILM)
Banks with a BI above EUR 1 billion must also apply an Internal Loss Multiplier
based on their 10-year average annual operational risk losses relative to the BIC.
The ILM ranges from 1x to 3x.

8.3 Operational Risk Capital Requirement
   OpRisk RWA = 12.5 × BIC × ILM (where applicable)
